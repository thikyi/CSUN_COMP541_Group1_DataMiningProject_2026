Inside Airbnb Accommodation Data Exploration

The Inside Airbnb accommodation dataset was used to measure short-term lodging supply,
nightly accommodation cost, listing availability, review activity, and room-type composition
across global travel destinations. The notebook collected public Inside Airbnb summary
listings.csv files from the Inside Airbnb data page and combined the files into one dataset.

The source collection identified 119 global Inside Airbnb areas.
The download step completed with 0 failed downloads. The raw
combined dataset contained 1,563,867 listing-level records and 30
fields. During cleaning, the price field was converted from text to numeric format, missing
or invalid prices were removed, and the top 1 percent of price values within each destination
group were removed as extreme outliers. The cleaned listing-level dataset contained
1,054,004 records and 27 fields. A destination-level
summary was then created with 103 destination records.

Because the data covers multiple countries, raw listing prices may be stored in local currencies.
To support fairer cross-destination comparison, destination-level price fields were converted
to USD. Most rates were obtained through the exchange-rate API. For Belize and Taipei, manual
source-currency-to-USD rates were added using user-provided market values as of June 16,
2026: 1 USD = 2.0142 BZD and 1 USD = 31.5695 TWD. This means the notebook used
1 BZD = 0.496475 USD and 1 TWD = 0.031676 USD.

For central tendency, the analysis emphasized median nightly Airbnb price rather than only
mean price because listing prices are right-skewed and luxury listings can pull the mean
upward. The estimated seven-day lodging cost was calculated by multiplying the destination
median nightly price by seven. This created a travel-focused lodging-cost feature that can be
used later in the Budget Value Index.

For dispersion, the analysis calculated minimum, maximum, range, quartiles, IQR, variance,
and standard deviation for listing-level and destination-level numeric fields. The spread of
prices confirmed that accommodation costs vary widely across destinations and room types.
The skewness analysis also showed that raw listing prices have a long right tail. For this
reason, the notebook generated both raw price histograms and log-transformed price
histograms.

The room_type field was used to check class imbalance. Entire home/apt listings were the
dominant category, followed by private rooms, while shared rooms and hotel rooms represented
much smaller shares of the data. This imbalance should be considered if room_type is used
later in modeling.

Pearson correlation analysis was calculated across destination-level numeric features. The
strongest expected relationship is between median Airbnb price and estimated seven-day
lodging cost because the seven-day cost is calculated directly from the median nightly price.
The correlation matrix also helps identify relationships among listing supply, review activity,
availability, and lodging cost before feature selection and modeling.

For merging, the recommended key is location_path because destination names alone may not
be unique across countries or regions. The main lodging-cost feature for the final merged
dataset should be estimated_7_day_lodging_cost_usd. The quality-checked merge-ready file
also includes ready_for_cost_merge as a flag showing whether a destination has a USD lodging
cost and enough listings for cost-based comparison.
