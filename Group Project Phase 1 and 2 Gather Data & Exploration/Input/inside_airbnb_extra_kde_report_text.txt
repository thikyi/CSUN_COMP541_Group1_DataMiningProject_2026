Additional Inside Airbnb KDE and EDA Findings

KDE density plots were added to provide a smoother view of the accommodation price
distributions. Because listing prices are strongly right-skewed, the main KDE plot uses
log-transformed listing prices. This makes the central mass of the distribution easier to
interpret while reducing the visual effect of luxury listings and extreme high-price values.

A separate KDE plot by room type compares Entire home/apt, Private room, Shared room, and
Hotel room listings. This helps show that room type affects the distribution of prices and
supports the earlier class-imbalance finding that Entire home/apt dominates the dataset.

Destination-level KDE plots were also added for median nightly price in USD and estimated
7-day lodging cost in USD. These are more useful for the travel recommendation task than
raw listing-level prices because the final project compares destinations rather than individual
listings.

Additional quality-control plots were added to show row retention after cleaning and area
coverage across processing stages. These figures help explain how many records and areas
remain after removing unusable prices and outliers. They also help justify why some areas
need additional review before final merging.

An exploratory accommodation value score was created for EDA only. This score combines
lower 7-day lodging cost, availability, review activity, and listing supply. It is not the final
Budget Value Index, but it helps identify destinations that may be strong accommodation
value candidates before the full multi-source merge.
